Why Found in Translation

Some of Latin America's most famous writers in the English language include Colombia’s Gabriel García Márquez, Chile's Pablo Neruda and Isabel Allende, or Peru's Mario Vargas Llosa.

But with over 600 million people spread over 21 different countries in the region, Latin American literature has much more to offer and readers from many English-speaking countries are missing out on this literary diversity and originality.

Fortunately, there are tremendously talented translators of Latin American and Caribbean writers. Found in Translation wants to introduce you to as many as possible.

Therefore, this space also sheds light on the art, challenges and benefits of translation by featuring information on the topic itself,as well as of translators behind the literary works.
